# AI Summary of Author's Pipeline
**Here is the chronological summary of the pipeline utilized by Durairaj et al. (2023), mapped directly to the software and repositories they published:**

### Step 1: Data Acquisition and Database Construction
Before any clustering begins, the raw protein universe must be gathered, filtered, and annotated to determine which proteins are "dark" (uncharacterized) versus "light" (characterized).
* Repository: `dbuilder` (Database Builder)
* Key Code: `extract_uniref.py`, `extract_uniparc.py`, `extract_alphafold.py`: Fetches base sequences and filters for AlphaFold structures with high confidence (pLDDT > 90).
    * extract_interpro.py, extract_uniprot.py: Gathers domain/family annotations and semantic descriptions to calculate the initial darkness metrics.
    * extract_dbs_with_mongo.py: Aggregates all this massive data into a unified MongoDB.

### Step 2: All-Against-All Sequence Alignment
The unified dataset of 53 million high-quality representative sequences is aligned against itself to establish the initial edges (connections) of the network.
* Software: MMseqs2 (release 13-45111)
* Key Parameters: The software executes a self-search where alignments must cover at least 50% of the sequence length with an E-value threshold of $1×10**−4$.

### Step 3: Community Detection and Graph Analysis
The massive sequence alignment network generated by MMseqs2 is simplified by grouping connected nodes into distinct "communities" and analyzing their properties.
* Repository: `AFDB90v4`
* Key Code:
    * `get_connected_components.py`, `get_communities_summary.py`, `make_communities_map.py`: Implements the Python NetworkX asynchronous label propagation algorithm to define the clusters and prepare them for Cosmograph visualization.
    * Jupyter Notebooks (e.g., `1. Analyse UniRef50 and AFDB darkness.ipynb`): Calculates the actual percentages of "dark" proteins per community and generates the statistical plots for the paper.
### Step 4: Structural Outlier Detection
To identify structurally novel proteins that might be missed by sequence alignment alone, the 3D structures of the AlphaFold models are mathematically decomposed and compared.
* Repositories and Tools: `geometricus`, `AFDB90v4`, and `Foldseek`
* Key Code:
    * `make_shapemers.py` (in AFDB90v4): Uses the `geometricus` library to mathematically convert 3D backbone coordinates into rotationally and translationally invariant "shape-mers".
    * FastText algorithm: Trains a neural network on these shape-mers to embed the proteins into a vector space and flag structural outliers (proteins with alien 3D geometry).
    * Foldseek: Used independently to query these outlier structures against the existing Protein Data Bank (PDB) to confirm they represent genuinely novel folds (such as the β-flower fold discovered in the study).

This complete architecture moves the data from raw text extraction (dbuilder), to sequence network building (MMseqs2), to graph clustering (AFDB90v4), and finally to 3D structural analysis (geometricus and Foldseek).
